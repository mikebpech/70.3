THE 6 RULES

🥩
160–175g protein every single day
This is the non-negotiable. At ~77kg doing 10–14hrs/week, 2.2g/kg protein preserves muscle while your body fat drops from the cardio. Miss your protein target and you'll look like a cardio bunny instead of an athlete. Chicken, salmon, eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, whey — rotate them all.
🍚
Time carbs around workouts, not random
Eat your biggest carb hits before and immediately after sessions. White rice, oats, banana, pasta, sweet potato — all fine. On rest days, cut carbs by 30–40% and let protein + fat fill the gap. You don't need 400g of carbs to sit on the couch.
😴
Sleep is your best fat-loss tool
8–9hrs non-negotiable. Poor sleep spikes cortisol which parks fat around your midsection. No diet trick beats it. The 6-pack you want is 60% training, 30% sleep, 10% diet discipline. Get off your phone at 10pm.
🍕
Saturday night is yours — earn it
After your long ride, eat whatever you want for dinner. Poutine, pizza, burgers, sushi. You've burned 1,200+ kcal on the bike and have a long run tomorrow — this is literally a refeed, not a cheat. One meal won't undo 13 weeks. Enjoy it mentally, not just physically.
🍺
Alcohol: max 2 drinks, never before a key session
1–2 drinks Friday or Saturday post-ride is fine. But alcohol tanks sleep quality for 2 nights, not one. Hard rule: zero alcohol Thursday (before Saturday long ride) and full race week. Everything else is your call.
🏊
Race nutrition is a skill — train it every Saturday
Your gut needs to learn to absorb 60–70g carbs/hr while exercising. Start practising now on every long ride. Test gels, bars, bananas, rice cakes. Race day is NOT the time to try something new. If your stomach can handle it in training, it'll handle it at Tremblant.

MEAL OPTIONS

Mix and match across the week. Easy ones are weeknight defaults — under 15 min. Harder ones are for when you actually feel like cooking. All hit ~45–55g protein per dinner.

⚡ Easy — Under 15 min
Chicken + Rice Pouch + Frozen Broccoli
90s microwave rice, air-fry or microwave pre-cooked chicken strips, dump frozen broccoli in microwave. Sriracha or soy sauce. Done in 8 minutes. Your staple weeknight meal — make peace with it.
Ground Turkey Taco Bowl
Brown 200g extra-lean ground turkey (7min), season with cumin + chili powder, serve over rice with salsa, black beans, lime. High protein, fun macros, takes one pan.
Tuna + Sweet Potato + Greens
2 cans tuna (drained), microwave sweet potato (5min), big handful of greens with olive oil + lemon. Zero cooking skill required. Omega-3s without the salmon.
Egg White Scramble + Toast
6 egg whites + 2 whole eggs scrambled, 2 slices sourdough, sliced tomatoes. Quick and cheap. Good on low-appetite evenings after a hard session when heavy food sounds awful.
Rotisserie Chicken + Anything
Grab a rotisserie chicken from IGA or Metro. Pull 200g of breast meat. Pair with whatever starch and veg you have. The laziest high-protein meal there is — and it's actually good.
Shrimp Stir-Fry + Noodles
200g frozen shrimp (thaw in 5min under cold water), stir-fry with garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, frozen veg mix. Serve over rice noodles. 10 minutes total.
🔥 Harder — Weekend cooking, worth it
Beef + Veggie Chili (Batch Cook)
500g extra-lean ground beef, 2 cans kidney beans, can of diced tomatoes, onion, garlic, chili powder. Simmer 30min. Makes 4–5 meals. Freeze half. High protein, high fibre, stupid easy to reheat.
Chicken Thighs + Roasted Root Veg
Season bone-in chicken thighs with garlic + paprika + olive oil. Roast at 425°F for 35min alongside cubed sweet potato and carrots. Hands-off cooking. Makes great leftovers for lunch.
Pasta Bolognese (The Post-Long-Ride Classic)
200g dry pasta, 200g extra-lean ground beef, jarred tomato sauce, parmesan. 20min total. This is your Saturday night go-to before the fun meal — high carb, high protein, crowd pleaser.
Turkey Meatballs + Quinoa + Sauce
Mix 400g ground turkey with egg, breadcrumbs, garlic, Italian seasoning. Roll into balls, bake 20min at 400°F. Serve over quinoa with marinara. Batch-friendly — make 20 meatballs, freeze half.
Steak + Sweet Potato Mash + Asparagus
200g sirloin or flank steak, pan-seared 3–4min per side. Mash sweet potato with a bit of butter. Roast asparagus 10min. Restaurant quality, 25 minutes. Good Saturday dinner when you want real food.
Greek Chicken Bowl (Meal Prep King)
Marinate chicken thighs in lemon + garlic + oregano + olive oil overnight. Grill or bake. Serve over rice with cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, tzatziki. Makes 4 lunches or dinners at once — best meal prep of the plan.
☕
Daily Non-Negotiable: Iced Latte with Maple
Keep it. One iced latte with a small pour of maple syrup is ~150–200 kcal and genuinely makes life worth living when you're training 12hrs/week. It's already factored into your daily targets. Use oat milk or 2% — both fine. Just don't make it a large double-double with extra maple three times a day and you're good.

RACE DAY NUTRITION

5:00am — Breakfast
500–600 kcal, 2hrs before swim
Oatmeal + banana + 2 slices toast with PB + 500ml water. Same breakfast you've been testing in training. No surprises on race day.
6:45am — Pre-swim
1 gel + 300ml electrolytes
15min before the swim start. Tops up blood glucose. Your last solid fuel before 40min in the water.
On the bike
60–70g carbs/hr, 500ml fluid/hr
Start eating at 20min. Gel every 30min + electrolytes. Aim for 2 bottles/hr. If you're not eating, you're falling behind. You've practised this every Saturday — execute it.
On the run
Gel at T2 + every aid station
Take a gel immediately at T2. Hit every aid station — cola + water from km 10 onwards if stomach allows. Walk the aid stations if needed. Don't be a hero.

SUPPLEMENTS — KEEP IT SIMPLE

Whey Protein
1–2 scoops/day
Hits your protein target conveniently. Take within 30min of any hard session. Don't overthink the brand.
Creatine Monohydrate
5g every morning
Yes, even for endurance. Supports power output on threshold bike sessions. 5g/day, no loading phase needed. Cheap and backed by 30 years of research.
Vitamin D3 + K2
2,000–4,000 IU/day
Montreal in spring means you're still D-deficient from winter. Supports immune function, bone health, testosterone. Take with breakfast (fat-soluble).
Electrolytes
Any session over 60min
Sodium, magnesium, potassium. Cramping on the run is an electrolyte problem, not just hydration. Use in training every Saturday — don't discover you need them on race day.
